# Design Checklist: Case Study

Anchored in Runeson & Höst (2009), "Guidelines for conducting and reporting case study research in software engineering," and the ACM SIGSOFT Empirical Standards (Ralph et al., 2021).

## Design
- [ ] **Purpose** classified: exploratory, descriptive, explanatory, or improving
- [ ] **Research questions** stated and answerable with case-study evidence
- [ ] **Case and unit of analysis** defined (project, team, organization, feature)
- [ ] **Single vs. multiple case** justified; for multiple cases, the replication logic (literal vs. theoretical) is stated
- [ ] **Case selection** rationale explicit (why this case; representativeness or revelatory value)
- [ ] **Theoretical frame / propositions** stated where the study is explanatory

## Data Collection
- [ ] **Multiple data sources** planned for triangulation (interviews, repository data, documents, observation)
- [ ] **Protocol** documented: what data, from whom, when, how
- [ ] **Interview guide** (if used) piloted; questions open and non-leading
- [ ] **Chain of evidence** maintained (traceable from question to data to finding)
- [ ] **Ethics/IRB, consent, and confidentiality** for participants and proprietary data

## Analysis
- [ ] **Coding scheme** defined; qualitative analysis method stated (thematic analysis, grounded theory, etc.)
- [ ] **Inter-rater reliability** for coding (Cohen's kappa) or documented consensus procedure
- [ ] **Triangulation** across sources performed and reported
- [ ] **Negative/deviant cases** sought, not just confirming evidence
- [ ] **Member checking:** findings validated with participants where feasible

## Threats to Validity
- [ ] **Construct:** do the measures/questions capture the intended concepts? (multiple sources, member checking)
- [ ] **Internal / credibility:** rival explanations considered; triangulation
- [ ] **External / transferability:** context described richly enough for readers to judge transfer; no over-generalization from one case
- [ ] **Reliability / dependability:** protocol and audit trail let another researcher follow the process

## Reporting
- [ ] **Rich context description** (organization, domain, scale) — enables transferability judgments
- [ ] **Quotes/evidence** support each finding
- [ ] **Researcher role and potential bias** disclosed

## Referee Objections to Anticipate
- "It's just one case" — argue revelatory/representative value; scope the claim
- "Not generalizable" — reframe as transferability with rich context
- "Cherry-picked evidence" — show the protocol, negative cases, and triangulation
- "Researcher bias" — describe reflexivity and member checking
